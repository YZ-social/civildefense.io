Safety First

Available

  • No app store can block Civil Defense—the app is just a web page. It can be installed to your home screen if desired.
  • Taking down web servers or domain names cannot block access for those who already have it, because the app caches everything on first use. The open-source code is available so anyone can host an alternative mirror.
  • There is no back-end server or data center to take down. All alert data is entirely peer-to-peer using Axona. All mirrors share this network—a post on civildefense.io appears on the mirror at ki1r0y.com and vice versa.
  • Blocking network traffic is extremely difficult, because the low-level data is identical to video conferencing traffic and does not pass through identifiable servers.
  • No user can be censored because there are no accounts. No one can steal your account information because there isn't any.

Anonymous

  • You cannot be identified as a person because there is no login, sign-up, or accounts. No phone number or email is used. There is no app store logging downloads.
  • It is extremely difficult to identify your IP address or network patterns, because alerts are distributed phone-to-phone using the Axona peer-to-peer network in a form identical to encrypted video conferencing standards.
  • Notifications are sent directly through the foreground app on your phone, without going through Apple or Google's messaging service. (Backgrounded app notifications available by Winter '26.)
  • Alerts disappear everywhere on the network after 24 hours. There are no records of calls or requested hashtags. An alert can only be associated with a device through physical access to that unlocked device—and only if the app data has not been wiped.
  • Users can reliably recognize postings from people with whom they've previously interacted, without knowing their actual names or phone numbers.

The Bigger Picture

The Civil Defense software was created by a group of experienced system developers who are also developing the Axona network that it runs on. Both are free and open source.

Axona is a modern descendant of the old peer-to-peer networks such as Napster. It has a unique self-healing routing mechanism that uses neuron-learning techniques to find the best way to distribute alerts—analogous to TOR (The Onion Router), but designed from the ground up to run in mobile and desktop browsers.

We believe there are many applications where developers and users alike benefit from open software and decentralized networks—without app stores, servers, or data centers—backed by modern cryptography. Beyond Civil Defense, we intend to develop large-scale multiuser collaboration and Inclusive Instant Payment Systems.
